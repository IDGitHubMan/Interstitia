Flows

Isaiah Desrosiers

A flow field is a surprisingly simple concept. Imagine, if you will, a grid of cells. You are moving through this grid, for some reason or other. But how is your movement defined? Simple. Each cell in the grid gives a direction, maybe go North, or South, or 3 units up, 2 down.

(10,30)

(7,1)

(1000,5)

(90,60)

(6,12)

(8,183)

This  is a basic flow field. Of course, the definition could be produced in countless ways. It could be entirely random like above. Or it could actually be based on something like Perlin Noise. Or even an equation.
